Why We NEED to Save the Penguins

Penguins are in danger for a variety of reasons. Many of those reasons are man made. No matter how much we need oil, we cannot look over the fact that oil spills spell disaster for animals. The Deepwater Horizon, an oil rig located in the Gulf of Mexico, exploded in April of 2010. In just 6 months over 8,000 animals died. Many of the animals that died were endangered. In 2011 a cargo ship carrying crude oil crashed on Nightingale Island in the remote British South Atlantic region. It ended up releasing over 800 tons of toxic fuel oil into the sea waters. That island was home to over 200,000 penguins. This territory contains nearly half of the world’s population of northern rockhopper penguins, a species that has been endangered for many years. This problem is one of many that needs to be fixed by the people who caused it to happen in the first.


What are the health risks asscociated with oil spills?

The birds are often in poor shape and dehydrated. When the penguin begins to preen its feathers in an attempt to remove the oil, the toxic material is ingested, causing damage to the bird’s internal organs. Surviving birds may be severely injured with oil coating their skin and feathers. It contains deadly chemicals that burns their skin. The sticky oil is heavy and affects the buoyancy of the bird. This makes it difficult for the bird to swim and move about freely. This could be dangerous if they need to get food as they could die from starvation.
